Example 3: (by text)


    Text="Hello"
    Pad="ACDBF":

    "Hello" + "ACDBF" = ....
    ..... - "ACDBF" = "Hello"




And now, using table we can encode our message.

Now when we use table position of signs (table above), we have to check the positions all the letters in our message.

You see, when we use the table above we can see the letters and the positions of letters -

H in your message is on the position number: 7
e in your message is on the position number: 4
l in your message is on the position number: 11
etc.

It is very easy you can do it even on piece of paper but be careful! We start count the letters from number 0, not 1, therefore the A is on the position number 0!


Message H  e  l l o
Position letter of message in Table1 7  4  11 11 14
PAD A  C  D B F
Position letter of PAD in Table1 0  2  3 1 5

Now we adding position of message letters and position of PAD letters

Position letter of message in Table1 7  4  11 11 14
Position letter of PAD in Table1        + 0  2  3 1 5
SUM 7  6  14 12 19

In the next step you have to check what is the letter on the SUM positions

SUM 7  6  14 12 19
Letter in Table1  H  G  o M T

And we obtain encrypted message: HGoMT

    Text="Hello"
    Pad="ACDBF":

    "Hello" + "ACDBF" = "HGoMT"
    "HGoMT" - "ACDBF" = "Hello"
